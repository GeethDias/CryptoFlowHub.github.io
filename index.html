CAESAR'S CIPHER

Caesar's cipher, attributed to Julius Caesar, is a basic form of substitution cipher. It involves shifting each letter of the plaintext by a fixed number of positions down or up the alphabet. This method offers limited security, as there are only 25 possible shifts. It's an early example of cryptography, used to encode messages with a straightforward and easily decipherable pattern.

STREAM CIPHER

A stream cipher is a cryptographic technique that encrypts data one bit or byte at a time. It involves combining the plaintext with a stream of random or pseudorandom key elements to produce the ciphertext. Stream ciphers are efficient for real-time communication and offer strong security when the key stream remains secret and doesn't repeat.
